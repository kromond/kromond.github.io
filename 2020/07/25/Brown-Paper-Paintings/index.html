Brown Paper Paintings

This project was borne out of a desperate need to make some big pictures. Making big pictures feels so great, but presents a practical issue which has been a problem in my life. I’m left with big paintings to manage. There is a quote I half rememember that goes something like this: “if it’s not sold, it’s not art. It’s a storage problem.”

My solution to this was to buy a role of brown paper. This has such a low cost, low impact on my life that it’s safe to fail. This is a great way to feel free to make something bad which might end up not so bad.

My subject for these will be sill frames from forgotton films which capture narrative moments. This one will be a slow burn. I’ll post these newest at the top.

This one I cracked out the airbrush. This was a tool I use to love but have not used in many many years because my compressor broke. I bought a cheap one and plan to keep working it into the practice.
